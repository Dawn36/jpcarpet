Effective Wood Floor Cleaning Tips

Nowadays, in most of the homes wood floor is the preferred options for both buyers and sellers. Whether they are old or recently installed, hardwood floors can run at a high price. For this reason, wood floor cleaning service is most important. At JP Carpet Cleaning Expert Floor Care, we understand all your needs. In this blog, you can learn how you can maintain it by performing wood floor cleaning. This could help you to keep the wooden floor in its excellent condition.

Wood Floor Cleaning Schedule

The ideal solution to protect your wooden floor is to perform a regular maintenance schedule.

This will help retain the integrity and performance of your hardwood floors for as long as possible. The following tasks can be completed frequently.

  • Sweep or dust mop on daily basis.
  • Vacuum you floor weekly.
  • Use a wood safe floor cleaner and clean the floor once in a month.

    • These tasks can be managed by yourself, but the next task will need a professional and should only be performed by experienced professional. Although the cost will be higher. Fortunately, these maintenance projects are needed infrequently, giving you time to plan a budget for them.

  • You need to have maintenance and coating in the duration of every 3 to 5 years.
  • Every few decades sand and refinish.

    • It is very important to prevent or avoid unnecessary damage while maintaining any floor surface or covering. You need to wipe up spills quickly, as wood is porous the standing liquid will get penetrated. Highly pigmented liquids can easily discolor the wood. For similar reasons, it is good if you avoid wet mop or steamer. If any excess water that is able to sit on the wood can eventually cause it to wrap or mold. Instead, use a damp cloth or microfiber towel. Microfiber towels are great at grabbing the smallest piece of dust and are recommended by experts for cleaning wood floors.

    Maintain It’s Curb Appeal

    When it comes to looks, avoid using a stiff broom to maintain its wood floor finish. It can create scratches, eventually leaving the wood boards exposed. Likewise, you should never use a vacuum with the spinning brush. Instead, use a soft bristle broom attachment. This will allow you to brush in between the crevices of the wood floor.
